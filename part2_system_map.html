Relationships & Calculations
Step 1 — Summation
Baseline Per-Person Use
All four category inputs are summed to produce total daily water use per person before temperature adjustment
Drinking + Sanitation + Cooking + Other
Step 2 — Temperature Scaling
Heat Adjustment Multiplier
A tiered multiplier is applied based on ambient temperature. Hot conditions increase per-person demand, reflecting physiological need and evaporative loss
Adjusted = Baseline × Temp. Multiplier
Step 3 — Population Scaling
Site-Wide Demand
Adjusted per-person use is multiplied across the full population range. Demand scales linearly — a key assumption of the model
Total = Adjusted Per-Person × N People
Step 4 — Time Scaling
Weekly & Monthly Projections
Daily totals are extended to weekly (×7) and monthly (×30) values to support planning decisions
Weekly = Daily × 7  |  Monthly = Daily × 30

Core Formula Total Demand (L/day) = ( Drinking + Sanitation + Cooking + Other ) × Temp. Multiplier × N People
Simplifying Assumptions
No transit losses Pumping always sufficient No supply limits Uniform per-person consumption Demand scales linearly with population Temperature is constant (single value) No seasonal variation in category use All workers on site simultaneously
Limits & Uncertainties
Real per-person use varies by individual Shift rosters reduce simultaneous occupancy Temperature fluctuates throughout the day Groundwater vs. piped supply reliability unknown Category defaults are estimates, not measured No accounting for water recycling on site
